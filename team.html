Project Director · PI
Martin Berger

Martin is an assistant professor at the Faculty of Archaeology and a member of the interdisciplinary research group Museums, Collections, and Society at Leiden University. His research bridges museum history, ethnography, and digital methodologies.

His interests include ethnographic museums and collection histories, indigenous forms of representation and agency, the impact of globalization on indigenous cultures, and the ethics of provenance research in decolonial contexts. He leads the overall research design of BECACO and manages partner relationships across 9 countries.

His own research contribution focuses on the post-WW2 art market and networks of trade for Latin American material, culminating in a synthetic monograph due in 2028.
