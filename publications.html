Workshop Paper BECACO Output ● New · 2026
2026
Generation of Multi-Modal Narratives of Cultural Objects from Knowledge Graphs and LLMs
Ishak Riali, Raphaël Gersen, Elizabeth Rodriguez Estrada, Gabriel Spautz Vieira, Martin Berger
Third International Workshop on Semantic Digital Humanities (SemDH 2026) · co-located with ESWC 2026 · Dubrovnik, Croatia
Proposes an automated system for generating contextualized narratives from structured museum data. Tabular object metadata (identifier, name, creator, culture, dating, material) is first transformed into a CIDOC CRM knowledge graph. Targeted Cypher queries extract contextual information per object, which is integrated into a structured prompt sent to an LLM to generate fluid, historically informed narratives. The output is then converted into an audio file for multi-modal playback. Validated empirically by domain experts (accuracy and relevance) and members of the general museum public (accessibility).

Conference Paper BECACO Output
2025
BecaGraph: Towards Collaborative Provenance Research Through Knowledge Graph Integration
Ishak Riali, Martin Berger
Digital Humanities Conference · Gdańsk, Poland · 2025
Introduces the BecaGraph pipeline — a purpose-built tool that transforms tabular museum collection data into CIDOC CRM-based knowledge graphs stored in Neo4j, with LLM-powered entity resolution for matching actors and objects across institutional datasets. The paper presents the architecture, the CIDOC mapping strategy, and preliminary results from integrating data from three partner museums.

Working Paper BECACO Output
2024
Entity Resolution in Cultural Heritage Datasets: A Large Language Model Approach
Ishak Riali, Martin Berger
BECACO Research Papers Series · Leiden University · 2024
Presents the design and evaluation of the entity resolution module at the heart of BecaGraph. The paper compares LLM-based semantic matching against traditional string-similarity approaches for identifying identical persons, places, and objects across heterogeneous museum database exports. Configurable similarity thresholds and their impact on precision/recall are analyzed across a test corpus of acquisition records from two Dutch museums.
